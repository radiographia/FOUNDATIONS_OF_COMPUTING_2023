Vision-Input Systems‌

A vision-input system consists of a digital camera to accept input by seeing an object. Input data in this case is normally an object's shape and features in the form of an image.

A computer with a vision-input device follows the following steps to recognize a given object:

1. It focuses the camera on the given object to take its picture.

2. The camera creates a digital image of the object and feeds it to the computer.

3. The computer matches the image with similarly formed pre-stored images in its image database.

4. The computer takes appropriate action depending on whether it finds a match or not.

Vision-input systems are often part of industrial robots that are used for quality control and assembly processes. For example, the robot of Figure 6.18 inspects the objects passing on a conveyor belt and separates those not meeting certain quality-control specifications. It also inspects the shapes of objects and separates objects of different shapes in different bins.
