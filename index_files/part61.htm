Digital Systems Design



Digital computers use several interconnected digital components, which in turn use digital circuits in the design. Logic gates and flip-flops are the building blocks of all digital circuits in a computer. As digital circuits conform to the arithmetic and logic operations of binary numbers, the algebra that deals with binary number system is used in the design of digital circuits. This algebra is called Boolean algebra.

This chapter deals with the design of digital systems. It introduces Boolean algebra, logic gates, and flip-flops. It then explains and illustrates with suitable examples how to use Boolean algebra, logic gates, and flip-flops to design digital circuits (both combinational and sequential).
