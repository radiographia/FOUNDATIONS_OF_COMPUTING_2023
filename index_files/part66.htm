Proof by the Method of Perfect Induction

We can also prove the theorems of Boolean algebra by means of truth tables. In a truth table, we need to check if both sides of the relation yield identical results for all possible combinations of variables involved. In principle, it is possible to enumerate all possible combinations of variables involved because Boolean algebra deals with variables having only two values. This method of proving theorems is called exhaustive enumeration or perfect induction.
