GEO-INFORMATICS‌

The word Geo-informatics (also called Geomatics in short) is composed of Geo (Earth), Information (Information Technology), and Matics (Geometry/measurement). Therefore, Geo- informatics is a multidisciplinary science, which combines knowledge of geo science, information technology, and mathematics. It is an arrangement of specialized computer hardware, software, and equipment used to acquire, manage, process, display, and disseminate spatial (concerning with space such as maps) and non-spatial (such as text) data in relation to geographic references. This data is integrated and analyzed to find useful patterns and trends of interest to the people and society to enable decision makers do effective planning and management. For example, image data of various parts of our earth collected by cameras fitted on satellites are processed, integrated, and analyzed to study our planet. Such studies are useful in effective planning and management of natural resources for their optimum utilization. They are also useful for predicting, controlling, and managing natural disasters, thus helping in minimizing damage to property and loss of precious lives. In carrying out this analytical study of data, knowledge of several specialized fields is utilized. These fields are:

a. Geographic Information System (GIS)

b. Remote sensing

c. Digital image processing

d. Cartography (the art of drawing maps and charts)

e. Photogrammetry (the art of using aerial photographs or satellite images to accurately measure distances between ground objects)

f. Terrain visualization (graphical representation in 2D or 3D form of a stretch of land with

its natural features)

g. Spatial data analysis and modeling

h. Content based image retrieval All these fields are part of Geo-informatics.
