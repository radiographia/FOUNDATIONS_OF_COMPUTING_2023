What is Bioinformatics?‌

The word Bioinformatics is composed of Bio (meaning Life Sciences) and Informatics (meaning Information Technology). Hence, bioinformatics deals with computer applications that solve problems of biology. However, not all types of computer applications that solve problems of biology fall into the area of bioinformatics. For example, the following types of computer applications do not fall into the category of bioinformatics:

1. Developing a database of ayurvedic medicines.

2. Developing a Decision Support System (DSS) that assists doctors in prescribing medicines to patients.

3. Using computers for fingerprints identification (this falls in the area of biometric security).

4. Using computers for factory automation in biotechnology industries.

5. Using computers to analyze ECG signals, X-ray images, MRI images, CT scan images, etc.

Therefore, the question now is “What type of computer applications for solving problems of biology fall into the category of bioinformatics?” The answer is - bioinformatics deals with only those problems of biology that involve data emerging from within cells of living beings. For example, the following types of computer applications fall into the category of bioinformatics:

1. Analyzing DNA (Deoxyribonucleic acid) sequence data to locate genes.

2. Analyzing RNA (Ribonucleic acid) sequence data to predict their structure.

3. Analyzing protein sequence data to predict their location inside cell.

4. Analyzing two or more bio-sequences (DNA or RNA or Protein) to find out their similarities to the maximum extent possible.

5. Using computational techniques to make classes, super-classes, and abstract classes of all organisms to study evolution of living beings.

6. Analyzing gene expression digital images.

7. Using computational techniques to minimize search time and effort for new drug molecules.

Most bioinformatics applications are compute intensive and require powerful supercomputers for their processing.
