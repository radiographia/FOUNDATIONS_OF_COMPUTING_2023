Cyber Forensics‌

To deal with criminals committing cyber crimes, law enforcement agencies have to investigate the evidences associated with such a crime, capture the culprit(s), and bring him/her into court of law. Obviously, like in other types of crimes, law enforcement agencies need efficient methods and procedures to investigate the evidences available to identify the culprit. Unfortunately, the evidences available in cyber crimes are not physical but digital in nature and require special techniques and methods for their collection, investigation, and presentation in the court of law. Moreover, digital evidences are volatile and prone to modification by others. Hence, the challenges are to acquire such evidences, analyze them, and to present them before the court of law in a manner that is acceptable by court for making decisions to prosecute cyber criminals. Cyber forensics is an area, which deals with such challenges. It deals with the acquisition, authentication, analysis, and documentation of evidences extracted from and/or contained in a computer system, computer network, computer media, or computer peripheral.

The two distinct areas of cyber forensics are:

1. Computer forensics. It deals with forensic analysis of computer system, and associated peripheral devices.

2. Network forensics. It deals with forensic analysis of network devices.
