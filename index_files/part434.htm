Basic Elements of a Communication System‌

Communication is the process of transferring a message from one point to another. Figure 12.1 shows the three basic elements (sender, medium, and receiver) of any communication system. For example, when you say ‘hello’ to your friend on phone, you are the sender, communication link between the two phones is the medium, and your friend is the receiver.

Similarly, in data communication, sender and receiver are normally machines (such as computers, terminals, peripheral devices like printers, plotters, disks, etc.), and transmission medium is a wired/wireless data communication channel (such as telephone lines, optical fiber, microwave links, satellite links, etc.).
