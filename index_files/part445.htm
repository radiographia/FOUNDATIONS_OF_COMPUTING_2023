Concentrators‌‌

A concentrator is an intelligent multiplexer, which performs the same function as a multiplexer, but has the ability to actually reduce the number of signals. For example, 100 signals from different devices coming into a concentrator could leave as 80 signals only. A concentrator does so by concentrating several signals to form a fewer number of signals. This requires intelligence, which normally takes the form of a microprocessor or even a minicomputer.

A communication system that uses a concentrator, connects devices of varying speeds and types to the concentrator, and connects the concentrator to a computer by a high-speed channel. Concentrators are useful where data communication cost is high, such as long-distance international communications.
