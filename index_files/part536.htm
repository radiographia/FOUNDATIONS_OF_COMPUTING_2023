POTENTIAL ATTACKS TO COMPUTER SYSTEMS‌

The first step in provisioning computer security is to identify potential threats/attacks to computer systems. A person or program trying to obtain unauthorized access to data or a resource of a computer system is known as an intruder or attacker. An intruder may attack a computer system in many ways. These attacks are broadly classified into two categories - passive attacks and active attacks. A passive attack does not cause any harm to the system under attack, whereas an active attack does. Therefore, passive attacks are inherently undetectable by the system and preventive measures are the only way to deal with them. On the other hand, systems handle active attacks by a combination of prevention, detection, and recovery techniques.
