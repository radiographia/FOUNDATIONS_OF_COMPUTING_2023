OPTICAL DISKS‌

An optical-disk storage system consists of a metallic or plastic disk coated with a reflective material. It uses laser beam technology for reading/writing of data from/to disk surface. Optical disks are also known as laser disks or optical laser disks because they use laser beam technology for data read/write. Computer systems use optical disks as a random access medium for high capacity secondary storage because of their capability to store large amount of data in a limited space.
