SWITCHING TECHNIQUES‌

In its simplest form, data communication takes place between two directly connected devices (nodes). However, it is often impractical or uneconomical to connect two devices directly. Instead, communication between a source node and a destination node often takes place by transmitting data from the source to destination through a network of intermediate nodes. These nodes provide switching facility for moving data from node-to-node until the data reaches its destination node. Three different methods of establishing communication links between a sender and a receiver in a communication network are circuit switching, message switching, and packet switching.
