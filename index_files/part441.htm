Analog versus Digital Transmission‌

Analog signals become weak and distorted when they travel long distances. Hence,

communication systems, which transmit analog signals, use amplifiers at periodic intervals along communication lines between modems to amplify (strengthen) weak analog signals.

Communication systems, which transmit digital signals, do not need modems. They use repeaters at periodic intervals along communication lines to strengthen digital signals, which become weak due to long distance travel.

For digital data, digital transmission is preferred to analog transmission because of:

1. Lower cost

2. No modems required

3. Higher transmission speeds possible

4. Lower error rate (higher reliability)
