Master-Slave Flip-Flop‌

A master-slave flip-flop is an interconnected circuit of two flip-flops in such a manner that the first one (master flip-flop) responds to the positive level of clock signal, and the second one (slave flip-flop) responds to the negative level of clock signal. The result is that the output changes during the transition of the clock signal from level 1 to level 0.

Figure 3.30 shows a master-slave D flip-flop created by connecting two D flip-flops in series, and inverting the clock signal input C for one of them. It behaves as a master-slave flip-flop because the second (slave) flip-flop in the series, changes only in response to a change in the first (master) flip-flop.
