Issues and Challenges in E-governance‌‌

The above listed benefits of e-governance require efforts far beyond mere computerization of government processes. In addition to technical challenges, its successful implementation requires fundamental change in conventional style of operation of government offices. The main challenge is to cope with re-engineering of government processes and new responsibilities of civil servants, businesses, and citizens.

Some of the technical challenges in successful implementation of e-governance are:

1. Creation of integrated information base. Creation of integrated information base requires the following:

a. Deciding what data to keep, how to capture it, and how to integrate data from multiple sources.

b. Building various types of data entry systems, which operate as near as possible to the source of data.

c. Validating and cleaning data, which is fragmented, out-of-date, and incorrect.

d. Removing inconsistency in data acquired from different sources.

e. Removing unwanted data.

2. Accessing of information. End users can make most effective use of data in the integrated information base, if it is universally and easily accessible. This requires the following:

a. Design and installation of access devices, which are easy to operate and located at easily accessible locations. Kiosks, push-button telephones, and access terminals are examples of few such devices.

b. Wide-area networking of such equipment with connectivity to the integrated information base.

c. Design of software that will enable different categories of users to visualize the information in a form useful and understandable to them.

3. Finding of right information. The integrated information base contains information of interest to many different types of users. Every user would like to access desired information from this large ocean of information in reasonable time. This requires necessary tools to properly structure and index the data to enable quick searching of any information by an authorized user.

4. Security and privacy of information. While e-governance allows information to be gathered, stored, and shared with greater ease, it also raises important issues of protecting information from unauthorized access to protect personal privacy. Hence, e-governance applications must implement proper user authentication and access control mechanisms to ensure that only authorized users can access some information. They must also use encryption techniques to safeguard tampering of critical information by unauthorized users when such information flows on communication channels.
