Points to Remember‌

1. Multimedia refers to the use of more than one of the following media for information presentation:

Text (alphanumeric characters) Graphics (line drawings and images) Animation (moving images)

Audio (sound)

Video (videographed real-life events)

2. A multimedia computer system is a computer having the capability to integrate two or more types of media (text, graphics, animation, audio, and video) for generation, storage, representation, manipulation, and access of multimedia information.

3. In general, data size of multimedia information is much larger than textual information. Due to this, multimedia computer systems require:

Faster CPU (for quicker processing of large amount of data), Larger storage devices (for storing large data files),

Larger main memory (for running programs with large data size),

Good graphics terminals (for displaying graphics, animation, and video), and

Audio I/O devices (for playing any audio associated with a multimedia application program).

4. Keyboards, electronic writing pads, OCRs, monitors, and printers are some commonly used hardware devices for processing text media.

5. Text editing, text searching, hypertext, and text importing/exporting are some desirable features of a multimedia computer system for better presentation and use of textual information.

6. Computer graphics deals with tools and technologies for generating, representing, manipulating, and displaying pictures (line drawings and images) with a computer.

7. Locating devices (such as mouse, joystick, or stylus), digitizers, scanners, digital cameras, computer screens with graphics display capability, laser printers, and plotters are some commonly used hardware devices for processing graphics media.

8. Desirable features of a multimedia computer system for better presentation and use of graphics information are painting and drawing software, screen-capture software, clip art, graphics importing, and software support for high resolution.

9. Computer animation deals with tools and technologies for generating, sequencing, and displaying (at a specified rate) a set of images (called frames) to create an effect of visual change or motion, similar to a movie film (video). Animation is commonly used in those instances where videography is not possible, or animation can illustrate a concept better than video.

10. Virtual reality is a technology, using which, a user can put on a pair of goggles and a glove and tour a three-dimensional world that exists only in a computer, but appears realistic to the user.

11. Scanners, digital cameras, video capture board interfaced to a video camera or video recorder, computer monitors with image display capability, and graphics accelerator board are some commonly used hardware devices for processing animation media.

12. Desirable features of a multimedia computer system with animation facility are animation-creation software, screen-capture software, animation clips, animation file importing, and software support for high resolution, recording and playback capability, and transition effects.

13. Computer audio deals with tools and technologies for synthesizing, recording, and playing audio or sound with a computer.

14. Transducer is a device that converts signals from analog to digital form or vice-versa.

15. MIDI (Musical Instrument Digital Interface) is an international standard for digital music that determines cabling, hardware, and communications protocol for connecting a computer to electronic musical instruments and recording equipment.

16. Sound-board, microphone, speaker, MIDI devices, sound synthesizer, sound editor and audio mixer are some commonly used hardware devices for processing audio media.

17. Desirable features of a multimedia computer system with audio facility are audio clips, audio file importing, software support for high quality sound, recording and playback capability, text-to-speech conversion software, speech-to-text conversion software, and voice-recognition software.

18. Computer video deals with tools and technologies for recording and displaying a sequence of images at a reasonable speed to create an impression of motion. Each individual image of such a sequence is called a frame.

19. The three main TV broadcasting standards are NTSC, PAL, and SECAM.

20. Video camera, video monitor, video board, and video editor are some commonly used hardware devices for processing video media.

21. Desirable features of a multimedia computer system with video facility are video clips, recording, and playback capability.

22. Some key sectors that have benefited with the advent of multimedia are education, entertainment, corporate, and science and engineering.

23. A few examples of multimedia applications are - multimedia books and e-books, digital library, multimedia presentation, foreign language learning, video games, special effects in movies, animation films, smart/interactive TV, virtual shops and shopping malls, multimedia kiosk, animated advertisements, multimedia conferencing, computer aided design, and visualization of experimental results.

24. Multimedia applications often use data compression techniques to reduce the volume of multimedia data. These techniques enable a multimedia application to compress multimedia data in such a way that the application can reproduce original data with desired quality level wherever needed.

25. The process of reducing data volume by applying a compression technique is called compression and the resulting data is called compressed data. The reverse process of reproducing original data from compressed data is called decompression and the resulting data is called decompressed data.

26. Data compression techniques may be lossless or lossy, symmetrical or asymmetrical, and software or hardware implemented.

27. The two commonly used lossless compression techniques are run-length coding and variable-length coding.

28. The two commonly used lossy compression techniques are predictive encoding and transform encoding.

29. Some standard multimedia compression techniques are:

JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group) for still images MPEG (Motion Pictures Experts Group) video for digital video MPEG audio for digital audio

30. Multimedia synchronization deals with mechanisms to ensure desired temporal ordering of a set of multimedia objects in a multimedia presentation.

31. The two levels of multimedia synchronization are temporal specification and temporal synchronization.

32. A media center computer is an electronic equipment for homes that can alone serve the purpose of all types of electronic entertainment equipment (such as television, music system, DVD/VCD player/recorder, videogame gadgets like playstation/gameboy, etc.) in addition to performing the jobs of a PC.
