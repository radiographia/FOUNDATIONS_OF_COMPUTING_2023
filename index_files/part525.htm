MULTIMEDIA SYNCHRONIZATION‌

Multimedia applications often involve simultaneous presentation of two or more types of media.

For example, a movie involves simultaneous presentation of video and audio. Similarly, a French movie with English subtitles involves simultaneous presentation of video, audio, and text. Such applications require multimedia synchronization for temporal ordering of a set of multimedia objects in a multimedia presentation. One such example is lip synchronization, which refers to the requirement that a speaker's voice should match the movements of his/her lips. Another example is subtitle synchronization, which refers to the requirement that timing of displaying a subtitle should match with the timing of corresponding video display (scene) and spoken words (voice).

The two levels of multimedia synchronization are temporal specification and temporal synchronization.
