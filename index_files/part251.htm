Documentation Standard‌

There have been many problems in the past with poorly documented software. Elimination of errors and incorporation of modifications are usually difficult in such software, making them expensive to maintain.

Realizing the importance of software documentation, several software organizations have developed strict documentation standards. These standards describe how to do documentation, how to choose meaningful program variable names, how to design the GUI for any software, how and up to what detail to include comments in program code, and what diagrams, charts, reports, outputs, etc. are necessary for completing documentation. Software developers and programmers working in such an organization are required to adhere to its documentation standards.
