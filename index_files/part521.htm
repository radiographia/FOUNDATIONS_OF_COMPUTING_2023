DATA COMPRESSION‌

Typically, multimedia data are large in volume and often require real-time and continuous delivery and presentation. Hence, multimedia applications require massive storage space, high data-transfer rate, and huge transmission bandwidth. Existing computers, storage devices, and networks are unable to satisfy these requirements. Hence, multimedia applications often use data compression techniques to reduce the volume of multimedia data. These techniques enable a multimedia application to compress multimedia data in such a way that the application can reproduce original data with desired quality level wherever needed.

The process of reducing data volume by applying a compression technique is called compression and the resulting data is called compressed data. The reverse process of reproducing original data from compressed data is called decompression and the resulting data is called decompressed data. Decompressed data may or may not be the same as original data depending on the type of compression technique.
