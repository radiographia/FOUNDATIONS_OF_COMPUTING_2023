Types of Wireless Computing Systems‌

Wireless computing systems are of two types:

1. Fixed wireless systems. These wireless computing systems support little or no mobility of their computing equipment. For example, a LAN can be set up by using wireless technology to get rid of the hassles of cables. The LAN will work as a conventional wired LAN, except that it does not need any cabling and its communication bandwidth may be much lower.

2. Mobile wireless systems. These wireless computing systems support mobility of computing equipment used to access the resources of wireless network. In turn, these systems support mobility of users and allow mobile users to access information from anywhere and at anytime. Resulting computing environment is called mobile computing or nomadic computing. Computing equipment that are commonly used in mobile wireless systems include laptops, smart phones, personal digital assistants (PDAs), and pagers with Internet access.
