Points to Remember‌

1. E-governance deals with IT applications that make operations in government offices better, cost less, and serve the needs of citizens and businesses more efficiently and effectively. Its goal is to create a more responsive, productive, and effective administration.

2. E-governance has several benefits for both the government and citizens. These include integrated information; integrated services; anywhere services; anywhere, anytime information; improved productivity; better decision making and planning; and better protection and security of information.

3. Some of the technical challenges in successful implementation of e-governance are - creation of integrated information base, accessing of information, finding of right information, and security and privacy of information.

4. Geo-informatics is an arrangement of specialized computer hardware, software, and equipment used to acquire, manage, process, display, and disseminate spatial (concerning with space such as maps) and non-spatial (such as text) data in relation to geographic

references.

5. Medical Informatics deals with computer applications in medical domain. The main objective of such applications is to optimize the acquisition, storage, retrieval, and use of healthcare data for offering better healthcare services.

6. Bioinformatics deals with computer applications to solve those problems of biology that involve data emerging from within cells of living beings.

7. Some of the main branches of bioinformatics are - Genomics, Proteomics, Computer- aided drug design, Data mining of bio databases, Molecular phylogenetics, Microarray data analysis, and Molecular modeling of biomolecules.
