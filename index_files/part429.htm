DATA WAREHOUSING VERSUS DATA MINING‌

It is important to know the difference between data warehousing and data mining. As Figure

11.16 shows, the two are related activities and reinforce each other. A data warehouse sets the stage for effective data mining. Users can perform data mining without a data warehouse, but using a data warehouse greatly improves the chances of success in data mining.

Data warehousing is knowledge verification, while data mining is knowledge discovery. Furthermore, data warehousing is multidimensional analysis, while data mining is multi-variable, single-dimensional analysis. For example, while data warehousing answers queries such as “What is the average credit granted to customers who pay their monthly bills in full versus those who choose to take financing?”, data mining gives attributes associated with customers who will take up financing.
