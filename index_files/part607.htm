Geo-informatics Applications‌

Geo-informatics applications mainly deal with natural resource management, environment management, infrastructure planning and management, land management, and disaster management. Few examples of such applications are:

1. Spatial Decision Support Systems (DSS). They are useful for effective planning and management of natural resources in applications such as:

a. Optimum utilization of agricultural land for maximum produce and forestry.

b. Wasteland management for disposal of hazardous waste from industrial areas.

c. Integrated planning of land and water resources.

2. Systems for planning and monitoring of resource distribution networks. Such systems are useful for planning and laying of a vast network of electricity cable, communication cable, or oil/gas/water pipeline. With such a system, officials in a control room can monitor the damages, leakages, or pilferages -all mapped and continuously watched over by earth-observing satellites.

3. Systems for infrastructure planning. Such systems are useful for planning new townships; planning of new facilities, roads, railway lines, etc. for existing towns and cities; and for organizing infrastructure for international games like Olympics, Asian games, and Commonwealth games.

4. Early warning systems. Such systems are useful for natural disasters like tsunamis, snow avalanches, floods, landslides, hurricanes, etc. They alert the people at risk before a natural disaster occurs so that they can evacuate to safer places, thus helping in minimizing damage to property and loss of precious lives.

5. Systems for micro-zonation and risk assessment of areas affected by natural disasters (hazards). Such systems use GIS and remote sensing techniques to prepare various thematic maps and overlays, and the risk assessment map (map showing hazardous zones). They also help in finding causative factors responsible for a natural disaster and suggest suitable mitigation measures through geotechnical parameters.

6. Systems for demarcation of potential zones for natural mineral and oil exploration. Such systems perform structural mapping of earth's surface using fine-resolution satellite data to identify potential zones where possibility of finding natural resources (such as gold, oil, etc.) is high. This helps in minimizing wastages involved in carrying out mineral exploration activities (like digging).

7. Systems for biodiversity characterization at landscape level to prioritize areas for conservation. Such systems use principles of landscape ecology to generate biodiversity maps with GIS and remote sensing tools. Biodiversity maps are useful in prioritizing areas for conservation using information on biodiversity, disturbance, and ecosystem uniqueness.

8. GIS-enabled websites. Google Earth, which supports detail map of almost all locations, is an example of such website.

Geo-informatics field is growing rapidly, primarily due to IT revolution and successful deployment of earth observing satellite systems. Several software packages like Geomatica, ARC/INFO, EASI/PACE, SPANS, PAMAP, ACE, OrthoEngine are available today that enable rapid development of geo-informatics applications. Some of these (like Geomatica) are complete geomatics solution, uniting technologies for remote sensing, image processing, spatial data analysis, photogrammetry, and cartography.
