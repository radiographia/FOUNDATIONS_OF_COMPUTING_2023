MEDIA CENTER COMPUTER‌

Multimedia applications have made computers a useful tool not only for office work, but also for education, entertainment, and archiving of digital data (personal photographs, songs, personal videos, etc.) at home. Hence, there is a growing trend of owning a personal computer (PC) at home like other electronic equipment (television, music system, DVD/VCD player/recorder, videogame gadgets such as playstation/gameboy, etc). In fact, users have coined new terminologies like “ infotainment” and “edutainment” to refer to computers as versatile tools for “information plus entertainment”, and “education plus entertainment”, respectively.

With increasing use of computers in homes, vendors came out with the idea of a single electronic equipment, called media center computer or media center PC, for homes. A user can purchase a media center computer for his/her home and get rid of all other electronic entertainment equipment like television, music system, DVD/VCD player/recorder, videogame gadgets such as playstation/gameboy, etc. because the functionalities of all these electronic equipment are built into a media center PC. Users can also use a media center PC as a normal PC for Internet access, office work, and other educational purposes. Contrary to the initial impression that people normally have, a media center computer just costs as much as a medium size television.

Microsoft's Media Center PC is called Windows Media Center PC.
