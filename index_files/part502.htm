INTERNET OF THINGS (IoT)‌

Kelvin Ashton, co-founder of MIT's Auto-ID Lab., was the first person to use the term Internet of Things (IoT) in one of his presentations in the year 1999. His idea was to enhance the scope of the Internet by connecting things, which can automatically collect data and communicate. Recall that the conventional Internet connects computing devices and depends on human beings to feed data to it. Hence, IoT is enhanced form of the Internet, in which the enhancement takes care of the following two objectives:

1. Interconnection of things, in addition to computing devices, and

2. Automatic collection and transfer of data, without any help from human being

Kelvin's vision behind proposing IoT was that, if we have an Internet that is able to gather and use data without any help from human being, we would be able to track everything, and greatly reduce wastage and loss, thus optimizing cost substantially.

Notice that the term Things in IoT refers to objects that can automatically collect data and communicate. Hence, any object (device) with any kind of built-in sensors and having the ability to collect and transfer data over a network without manual intervention, is a “Thing” in IoT, also referred to as smart objects. IoT, therefore, extends the Internet to interconnect smart objects as

well, and has the capability to see, hear and smell the world to gather information on its own and take decisions or help us take decisions.
