E-MAIL SECURITY‌

E-mail is a popular means of communication today. An e-mail message often travels through insecure communication channels and through several intermediate systems on its way from sender's system to receiver's system. A sensitive e-mail message requires that:

1. No one other than the intended recipient(s) should be able to read its contents.

2. No one should be able to tamper with its contents while it is traveling from the sender's system to the receiver's system, and the receiver should be able to make out if anyone tampers with the message.

3. It has indeed been sent by the intended sender.

Another issue in e-mail communication on the Internet is to protect the users from unsolicited (unwanted, not asked for) e-mails.

Some commonly used mechanisms/tools to deal with these issues are described here.
